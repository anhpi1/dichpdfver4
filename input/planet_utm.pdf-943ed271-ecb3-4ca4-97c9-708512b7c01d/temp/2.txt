Universal Transverse Mercator (UTM) Projection [5]
The UTM projection divides the world into 60 zones or segments. Each zone covers 6 degrees of longitude. The first zone starts at the international date line and the numbers increase eastwards. At the center of each zone is a central meridian, which is the measurement reference for that zone. [6]
East-west grid lines are attached to the central meridian at right angles and follow great circle paths away from it. These are lines of constant northing and do not follow parallels of latitude, since parallels of latitude are not great circles. Finally, north-south grid lines are draw as lines of constant easting. [7]
UTM coordinates are relative to a particular zone number and are expressed as an easting or X coordinate and a northing or Y coordinate. The easting is the distance from the central meridian along the east west grid lines using a positive east negative west sign convention. To avoid negative numbers, a false easting of 500,000 meters is assigned to the central meridian. [8]
The northing is the distance from the equator along the north-south grid lines using a positive north - neg- ative south sign convention. To avoid negative numbers in the southern hemisphere, a false northing of 10,000,000 meters is assigned to the equator. This false northing creates an ambiguity in this coordinate system. Consider the UTM coordinates: [9]
zone = 12, easting = 575 kilometers, northing = 5600 kilometers. [10]
Using the WGS84 datum, this converts to: [11]
latitude 50 32 49.64 N and longitude 109 56 29.12 W in the northern hemisphere or [12]
latitude 39 44 47.78 S and longitude 110 07 28.56 W in the southern hemisphere [13]
To resolve this ambiguity, the Pathloss program adds the suffix N or S to the UTM zone. e.g. 12N or 12S. This false northing introduces an additional complication when a terrain data base spans the equator. North- ings of 20 and 9980 kilometers represent points 20 kilometers north and south of the equator respectively. [14]
Planet Terrain Database Description [15]
The Planet terrain database format consists of regular array of elevations. Each elevation represents the av- erage elevation in a square cell. The file is arranged in rows running from west to east starting at either the SW or NW corner of the file. Normally the elevations are stored as 2 byte integers. The byte order could be INTEL (little endian) or SPARC (big endian) [16]
The geo-referencing information is contained in two external files: an index file and a projection file. [17]

UTM zone methods [39]
In many cases, terrain data is supplied in a single file. The east and west edges may ex- tend outside of the standard UTM zone bou- daries. In these cases, the UTM zone must be specified by either of the following two methods: [40]
Check the "Use specified index file" option and then select the file from the dropdown list. The UTM zone associated with the se- lected file will be used and only the selected file will be used even if the index contains multiple files. [41]
Check the “Use specified UTM zone” and enter the zone number suffixed by N or S. If N or S is not spec- ified the zone will default to the northern hemisphere. The S suffix must be specified for the southern hem- isphere [42]
In all other cases use the “Use standard UTM zones in index” option. [43]
Embedded building data [44]
Normally an elevation is interpolated from the nearest four elevations to the point of interest. If the terrain data contains embedded building or canopy data, check the “Embedded building data” option. The cell el- evations will be used in this case. [45]

Equatorial Considerations [84]
Terrain databases which span the equator require special consideration. Two index files are shown be- low. Both of these consists of four terrain data files covering an area of 20 kilometers by 20 kilometers. Two of these files extend 20 kilometers above the equator and the other two files extend 20 kilometers below the equator. [85]
The first index is referenced to the northern hemi- sphere. The map edge northings are +20,000 meters, 0 and -20,000 meters. In this case, a northern hemi- sphere UTM zone must be specified in both the in- dex and the UTM zone method [86]
The second index is referenced to the southern hemisphere. The northing of the equator is 10,000,000 me- ters.The map edge northings are 9,980,000 meters, 10,000,000 meters and 10,020,000 meters. In this case, a southern hemisphere UTM zone must be specified in both the index and the UTM zone method [87]
